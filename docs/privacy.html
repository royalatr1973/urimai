Urimai — Privacy Policy

உரிமை · Helping citizens discover the government welfare schemes they are entitled to.

Last updated: July 2026

Who we are

Urimai (“we”, “the service”) is an eligibility-discovery assistant for public welfare schemes, currently focused on schemes offered by the Government of Tamil Nadu, India. This service is in a limited testing phase and is not yet a production offering.

What we collect during discovery

During the discovery conversation — the part where we help you find out which schemes you are eligible for — we collect no identity information. We do not ask for or store your name, Aadhaar number, address, or exact phone number for this purpose. We collect only the facts required to check eligibility (for example: age, gender, state of residence, marital status, whether you have a regular income, disability percentage, and the presence of specific household disqualifiers such as land holdings above a threshold).

How WhatsApp messages are handled

If you contact Urimai through WhatsApp, the WhatsApp phone number the message came from is used strictly to route replies back to you and to remember your conversation state for a short window (up to 30 minutes of inactivity). We do not sell or share WhatsApp numbers, and we do not use them for marketing.

What we collect only at the apply stage

If — and only if — you choose to apply for a specific scheme through Urimai, we collect the identity information the scheme requires (such as your name, Aadhaar number, address, and contact details). This information is encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM and is used only to prepare or forward your application. It is retained for the minimum period necessary and is never sold.

Speech (voice notes)

If you send a voice note, its audio is transcribed to text via a speech-to-text provider (Bhashini or Sarvam AI). Only the transcribed text is retained by Urimai; the audio file is used only to produce that transcript.

Language models

The free text you send is passed to a large language model (Anthropic Claude) which extracts structured facts from it. Neither identity information nor apply-stage personal data is passed to this model. The model does not decide your eligibility — a separate, deterministic rules engine does.

The audit log

Each eligibility evaluation is recorded in an immutable audit log so that any verdict can be explained and reviewed. The audit log contains only the discovery facts (never identity information) plus the rule version used and the resulting verdict.

Your rights

You can request access to, correction of, or deletion of any personal information you have provided to Urimai during the apply stage. You can also ask us to stop processing your data at any time by contacting us at the address below. Because the discovery stage does not store identity, there is nothing personal to delete from it.

Children

Urimai is not intended for use by children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

We will update this page when the service changes materially. The “last updated” date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.
